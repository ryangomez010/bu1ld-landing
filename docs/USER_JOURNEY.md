# User Journey

Updated: 2026-07-19

## Canonical BU1LD journey

The BU1LD should optimize for one serious institutional path:

visitor → applicant → accepted contributor → project match → milestone assignment → artifact submission → review → public proof

## Public journey

1. A visitor lands on the public site and immediately understands that The BU1LD is a research-and-building institution, not a generic social community.
2. The visitor browses projects, labs, publications, competitions, programs, and evidence.
3. The visitor sees proof expectations: owners, milestones, contributors, evidence links, review state, and public outputs.
4. The visitor applies to a specific program or contribution pathway.

## Member journey

1. A member signs in and completes onboarding/profile setup.
2. The member defines technical expertise, interests, research/building capacity, and contribution preferences.
3. The member discovers projects by lab, field, difficulty, evidence status, and fit.
4. The member requests to contribute with a substantive proposal.
5. A project lead or administrator reviews the request.
6. Once accepted, the member works against milestones and submits artifacts.
7. A reviewer or project lead validates the contribution.
8. Approved outputs become part of project evidence/public proof where publication rules allow.

## Administrative journey

1. Admins publish and update labs, programs, projects, reviews, events, and evidence.
2. Admins review applications, moderate content, and handle reports.
3. Admins verify institutional claims only when evidence URLs support them.
4. Admins use audit logs and release checks before production changes.

## Non-goals

- Social feeds without contribution output.
- Inflated membership or partnership claims.
- Generic AI community mechanics that do not advance research, startup building, or public proof.
