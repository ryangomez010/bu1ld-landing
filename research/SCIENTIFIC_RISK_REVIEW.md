# Scientific Risk Review

## Highest-risk claim classes

1. **Novel intelligence mechanisms.** Genesis claims around developmental modular learning must be compared with dynamic sparse training, mixture-of-experts routing, progressive networks, continual learning, EWC, SI, replay, and modular routing baselines.
2. **Economic intelligence.** GenesisE can claim synthetic simulation behavior, not market validity, alpha, investment advice, or macroeconomic truth.
3. **Institutional credibility.** BU1LD must not claim affiliations, member counts, successful startups, publications, or partnerships without evidence rows and public source links.
4. **FinanceMeta outcomes.** FinanceMeta must not imply user financial success or investment recommendation quality without external evidence and compliance review.

## Invalid or weak evidence patterns to reject

- Single-seed smoke result presented as benchmark.
- Synthetic result presented as external validation.
- Ablation that removes a mechanism but also changes compute/capacity unfairly.
- Test-set inspection during architecture selection.
- Placeholder partnerships, fabricated users, or unsupported institutional names.
- “Runs locally” without saved config, seed, environment, and artifact hash.

## Kill criteria

- Genesis flagship: archive or narrow claims if matched baselines beat or match under fixed compute across five seeds.
- GenesisE real-market extension: do not proceed unless a real dataset protocol and no-investment-advice boundary are written first.
- BU1LD/FinanceMeta release: block if RLS denial tests or manual role smoke tests fail.
