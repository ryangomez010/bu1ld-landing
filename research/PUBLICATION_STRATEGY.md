# Publication Strategy

## Project Genesis

Target a reproducibility-first technical report before any venue submission. The report should claim only:

- mechanisms implemented;
- protocols run;
- artifacts produced;
- statistically supported comparisons.

Do not claim self-developing intelligence, open-ended architecture synthesis, or serving speedups from the current Python implementation.

## GenesisE

Position as a synthetic economic intelligence simulation toolkit. A paper can discuss software architecture, artifact contracts, and synthetic counterfactual methodology. Do not submit as empirical finance/economics evidence without external datasets and domain baselines.

## BU1LD and FinanceMeta

Publish as product case studies only after live role journeys and public evidence claims are verified. Avoid university/partner/customer claims unless linked in the evidence register.

## Portfolio consolidation

The next public-facing portfolio should contain fewer, stronger releases:

1. Project Genesis flagship research release.
2. BU1LD institutional platform.
3. FinanceMeta finance learning/member platform.
4. GenesisE synthetic economics lab.

Everything else remains private, archived, or explicitly conceptual until code/artifacts exist.
